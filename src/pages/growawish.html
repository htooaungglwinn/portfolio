The Goal

Reimagining usability and delivering positive visceral reactions to decrease drop off rates...

Understanding, Donald Arthur Norman’s, the UX design legend, methods of unlocking cognitive responses, a successful website is not just about aesthetics; it's about forging a connection with your audience, understanding their behaviors and more importantly understanding their emotions.

The Problem

“Don’t make users think!” - Don Norman

After conducting a quick content audit in the original site, I found that the calls to actions weren’t encouraging , the informational organizations were not very clear and the navigation was loaded with so much information.

So, it wasn’t very efficient for users. the users have to think and figure things out to navigate. So, how might we turn the site into a more efficient and engaging environment?

The Lading page

Provoking positive visceral reactions will lead to favorable calls to actions or interactions

The landing page that people see first is the most vital and challenging aspect in a website. It can keep the site visitors or drop them at hand. So, here are some improvements that were considered to improve user drop off rates.

Key Improvements: Fostering a visceral connections with the viewer by displaying a collection of actual product images as a hero image. Having the only call to action that is for ordering cards. A clear visual hierarchy between the big title and the subtitle.

Grouping related content to improve overall user comprehension and experience.

From the Gestalt’s principles: Proximity, information that are close to each other are perceived as similar or related. In the original landing page, call to actions that didn’t serve the same functionality were grouped together. So, changes could be made as following.

POS Section

The Point of Sale is pivotal for businesses selling products, driving revenue growth. Recognizing its importance, it should be displayed directly on the landing page. This ensures transparency for visitors, fostering confidence and contributing to the success of the startup.

(Redesigned POS Section)

Brand Mission

Grow A Wish is a brand with an inspiring and positive mission so, having the brand mission front and center on the landing page will not only reduces users' cognitive load but also establishes a lasting and positive brand impression, encouraging a deeper connection with its audience.

(Redesigned Brand Mission)

Footer Section

A few minor design changes have been implemented in the remaining sections of the landing page. In the footer, the hierarchical levels between the calls to action, headers, and text have been improved. This enhancement contributes to better readability and efficiency when users are scanning information on the site.
