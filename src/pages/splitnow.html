The Problem

Splitting bill among friends takes time and can often lead to stressful situations

When dining out with friends and dividing the bill, particularly when unfamiliar acquaintances are present, the procedure often becomes time-consuming and occasionally stressful. So, how can we address this challenge effectively?

Project Outline

Attitudinal Research

Splitting bills in real life can be time-consuming due to a LACK OF TRANSPARENCY in communication between the involved parties.

After carrying out five individual user interviews and then conducting thematic coding analysis, we have come to identify trends and themes on our user’s bill splitting experiences.

Key Insight 01: User’s bill splitting behaviors

  • Splitting equally is not always the case
  • Users want the app to accept the total and split evenly
  • And, users also expect to be able to split individually as well

Design solution: Give users options to split the bill evenly or edit each party split amount and roll back to splitting equally without further manual input. Let users remove and add more parties amid the procedure

Key insight 02: The Awkwardness of verbal communication

  • Splitting the bill requires further communications among parties
  • Reminding friends to repay their share can also lead to social insecurities. 
  • Currently, there's no way to remind friends to pay back other than through verbal communication.

Design solution: Having the system take care of asking back the money from a friend will remove social insecurities and thus maintain both parties' positive relationships and there will be no awkward verbal communication.

Key insight 03: The Process of splitting bills

  • The process of splitting the bill doesn't end immediately, as some people like to repay later
  • However, following up to payback isn’t always necessary between friends
  • Splitting bill takes time even within a group of friends and collages

Design solution: Streamline the bill splitting experience. Make a transaction first to pay the total and collect each friend's share afterwards. Keep the split record to see if all the parties have completed their parts of the bill.

User Testing + Improvements

Next the mid-fidelity variants were discussed over a cup of coffee with my mentor to prepare prototypes for user testing. We then carried out 4 user testing sessions. The results were then analyzed and found that there were two major roadblocks in our app.

Improvement 01: Simplified Home layout and improved contrast to enhance navigation

  • Splitting the bill requires further communications among parties
  • Reminding friends to repay their share can also lead to social insecurities. 
  • Currently, there's no way to remind friends to pay back other than through verbal communication.

Improvement 02: A more efficient “editing” interaction flow

  • Users were confused that “edit icon” was to change the price of each friend
  • Switching the “edit icon” with a “radio” for removing friends to eliminate confusion 
  • Also removing an extra step left us with a more efficient editing flow in our app
